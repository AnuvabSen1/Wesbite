About Me

I am a first-year PhD student at Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, USA, in the College of Electrical and Computer Engineering. My research interests include artificial intelligence and energy-efficient design, with a focus on building smart, energy-efficient models under tight resource constraints.

I graduated with a B.Tech in Electronics and Telecommunication from IIEST Shibpur in 2024. As an undergraduate, I engaged in diverse research experiences and received prestigious awards—including the Mitacs Globalink Research Scholarship, IEEE GRSS Travel Grant, and IEEE CIS travel grants.

I worked with Prof. Subhamoy Maitra at the Indian Statistical Institute on NMR Quantum Computing and quantum algorithm optimization, and collaborated with Dr. Subhabrata Roy for my undergraduate thesis on attention mechanisms in medical diagnosis and quantum machine learning.
