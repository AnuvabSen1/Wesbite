About Me

I am a first-year PhD student at the Georgia Institute of Technology in the College of Electrical and Computer Engineering. My research focuses on artificial intelligence and energy-efficient design, aiming to build smart models under tight resource constraints.

I graduated with a B.Tech in Electronics and Telecommunication from IIEST Shibpur in 2024. Throughout my undergraduate studies, I engaged in diverse research experiences and received prestigious awards including the Mitacs Globalink Research Scholarship, IEEE GRSS, and IEEE CIS travel grants.

Under the guidance of Prof. Subhamoy Maitra at the Indian Statistical Institute, I explored NMR Quantum Computing and quantum algorithm optimization, and collaborated with Dr. Subhabrata Roy on attention mechanisms for medical diagnosis and quantum machine learning.
